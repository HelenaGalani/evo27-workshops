What makes the EVO syllabus different

Not a traditional syllabus

The EVO syllabus is a learning roadmap, not a course policy document

📄 Traditional syllabus

Instructor info and office hours
Course overview and topics
Required textbooks and materials
Grading policy and breakdown
Schedule of exams and deadlines
Focus: What the course covers and how students are graded

🗺 EVO session syllabus

Weekly focus and progression
Weekly learning objectives
Tasks that support the objectives
Tools chosen for pedagogical purpose
Interaction type and workload
Focus: How learning happens, week by week, toward the session outcomes

How your syllabus is evaluated

The vetting rubric: what vetters look for

13 criteria, scored 0 to 3. You need 32/39 (80%) for acceptance.

3 Session outcomes

Clear, measurable, achievable, relevant for a 4-week EVO session

3 Weekly objectives

Specific, measurable, achievable, and support the session outcomes

3 Weekly tasks

Meaningful, active, clearly connected to the weekly objectives

3 Workload and feasibility

Realistic, balanced across four weeks, manageable for participants

3 Learning spaces and tools

Clear purpose, directly support objectives and tasks

3 Alignment and coherence

Logical progression, clear weekly focus, sequence supports learning

Key insight: The rubric does not just evaluate individual components. It evaluates how well they connect. A syllabus with clear objectives but disconnected tasks will score lower on alignment, even if each part looks fine on its own.
